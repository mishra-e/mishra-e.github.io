Academics

Research

Throughout my undergraduate studies, I worked at the University of Toronto's Mishra Lab for Mathematical Modelling under the supervision of Drs. Sharmistha Mishra and Jesse Knight. Together, we examined progress on the UNAIDS' 'first 95 goal' (to have 95% of those living with HIV know their status) among Female Sex Workers living with HIV across Sub-Saharan Africa.

Through my work with the Mishra Lab, I became interested in resource scarcity and healthcare equity research. Since then, I have focused my research on health economics issues, to understand better how systemic change may improve equitable access to care. I began working with Dr. Michel Grignon on two projects:

First, we are examining William Baumol's 'cost disease' in the healthcare systems of OECD countries across Europe and North America. The accurate diagnosis of this macroeconomic disease could explain rising healthcare costs in rich countries.

Second, under the supervision of Drs. Michel Grignon and Byron Spencer, I am conducting a retrospective study of mortality trend differentials in Canada, using the age-period-cohorts model. This project is funded by an Undergraduate Student Research Award.
